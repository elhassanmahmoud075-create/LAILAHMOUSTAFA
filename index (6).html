About

Laila Hussein Moustafa is Associate Professor of Library Administration and Middle East and North African Studies Librarian at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, where she also holds an appointment as Adjunct Faculty in the School of Information Sciences. She has served in key leadership roles, including Acting African Studies Librarian, and has built a sustained record of contributions to area studies librarianship, archival practice, and international scholarship.

Moustafa's scholarship addresses cultural heritage preservation, archives in contexts of conflict, and the role of libraries in sustaining knowledge production under conditions of displacement and political instability. Her publications have appeared in peer-reviewed venues including The American Archivist, Archivaria, Library Management, and RBM, and examine disaster planning, archival absence, and the reconstruction of knowledge infrastructures in the Middle East and other conflict-affected regions.

Her work further engages digital preservation, data ethics, and emerging technologies, including the implications of artificial intelligence for cultural heritage and academic libraries. She has delivered invited lectures and keynote presentations in national and international venues, including programs organized by UNESCO, IFLA, and the U.S. Department of State.

A Fulbright Specialist, Moustafa has an established record of professional service and leadership, including roles with the Middle East Librarians Association, the Endangered Archives Programme at the British Library, and IFLA. Her record of scholarship, international engagement, and professional leadership demonstrates sustained contributions to the fields of library and information science and Middle East and North African studies and reflects a profile of national and international standing.
